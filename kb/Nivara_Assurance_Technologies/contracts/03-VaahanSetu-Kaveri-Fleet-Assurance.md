## 1. Purpose and deployment scope

Kaveri Fleet Assurance appoints Nivara to provide the **Enterprise** configuration of VaahanSetu for fleet quotation, driver roster updates, claims-notice routing, and renewal worklists. The initial operating territory is Tamil Nadu, Kerala, and Andhra Pradesh. The parties expect the deployment to support shortening fleet-addition turnaround without bypassing approvals.

The agreed integration landscape includes fleet management export, claims gateway, and broker submission channel. Any connection that requires a third-party approval, source-system change, or additional security review will be tracked as a named implementation dependency rather than assumed to be available.

## 2. Term and commercial schedule

The initial term is **24 months** from the effective date. Subscription fees are **₹47.4 lakh per year**, invoiced **monthly in advance**, exclusive of applicable taxes. One-time discovery, configuration, migration, or travel work—if agreed—will be described in a separately approved statement of work.

At least 60 days before the term ends, the Account Director will convene a renewal review covering usage, service performance, planned scope changes, and pricing for a further period. Either party may decline renewal by written notice at least 45 days before expiry.

## 3. Delivery and acceptance

Launch is gated on named business owners, completed access setup, agreed interface tests, and a rollback plan. Nivara will support knowledge transfer so routine operating work remains with the Client's authorised team.

The Client will provide timely access to authorised subject-matter experts, test users, approved policy rules, and non-production interface materials. Acceptance is based on the mutually agreed scenario set, which must include incomplete evidence, manual referral, duplicate-event, and interface-delay cases relevant to this scope.

## 4. Service and governance

Standard service coverage is **24×7 priority intake; standard support 09:00–18:00 IST**. High-severity production incidents are acknowledged through the agreed support channel and handled according to the incident runbook. Planned maintenance and release windows will be communicated in advance wherever reasonably possible.

The parties will use a service-review pack that records availability, data-quality exceptions, unresolved dependencies, and user feedback. Material risks receive an owner, due date, and status at the next review.

## 5. Data, security, and confidentiality

Each party will protect confidential commercial and operational information. Production access follows least-privilege principles, while exports and privileged actions are auditable and subject to the agreed support process.

The Client controls its policy, customer, and operational source records. Nivara processes data only for the configured service and agreed support activities. Neither party may use the other party's confidential information outside this agreement without written permission, except where required by applicable law.

## 6. Product-specific commitment

During the first operating year, the parties will prioritise **a configurable fleet-risk dashboard for transport operators**. This priority is a service-design commitment, not a guarantee of an insurance outcome, claim decision, underwriting decision, or regulatory approval. Any material product expansion follows Nivara's documented change-control process.

## 7. Limitation and closure

To the extent permitted by applicable law, each party's direct liability under this fictional reference agreement is limited to subscription fees paid or payable in the preceding 12 months. Neither party is liable for indirect or consequential loss. On termination, the parties will follow an agreed export, access-removal, and transition plan.
