# Nivara Assurance Technologies Private Limited

## Who we are

Nivara Assurance Technologies Private Limited is a fictional, India-wide insurance-technology company founded in 2018. Its purpose is to help insurers, brokers, TPAs, reinsurers, and distribution partners run faster, more transparent insurance journeys without flattening India's regional and operational complexity into a generic workflow.

## Founding team

Nivara was co-founded in Bengaluru by **Aditi Chawla (Chief Executive Officer)**, **Samar Prakash (Chief Technology Officer)**, and **Maya Krishnan (Chief Product Officer)**. The three had worked alongside insurer operations teams in different capacities and kept encountering the same practical failure: policy and claims work was being digitised in fragments, leaving staff to reconcile spreadsheets, calls, documents, and disconnected systems.

- Aditi brought enterprise-partnership and operating-model experience. She became the company’s CEO and remains accountable for strategy, client commitments, and the discipline to grow without outrunning delivery.
- Samar brought platform and integration experience from large, distributed Indian services. As CTO, he sets the technical direction for secure, observable systems that can coexist with clients’ established technology.
- Maya brought service-design and product-research experience from consumer and regulated workflows. As CPO, she turns field evidence into a roadmap that is useful to both customers and the people who operate insurance every day.

Their founding decision was not to build a generic “AI for insurance” layer. Instead, they built Nivara around configured workflows, visible human decisions, reliable hand-offs, and India-specific service realities. This is fictional background material created solely for this reference corpus.

Nivara works from a distributed operating model: Bengaluru is the registered headquarters and product hub; Mumbai houses carrier partnerships and reinsurance relationships; Gurugram leads distribution and enterprise sales; Pune operates cloud reliability and data engineering; Chennai supports implementation; and Kochi coordinates multilingual service design. The company has 36 employees and 36 active client agreements.

All names, organisations, amounts, and operating examples in this corpus are fictional. They are designed to be coherent reference data, not descriptions of real businesses or people.

## Operating principles

1. **Indian by design.** Product rules account for state-wise distribution practices, PIN-code based risk signals, multilingual communication, UPI and bank-transfer reconciliation, and paper-assisted journeys where they still matter.
2. **Human decisions stay visible.** Models may prioritise, flag, and recommend; underwriting, claims, and exception decisions remain attributable to named customer teams.
3. **One policy story, many systems.** Nivara integrates carrier core systems, broker desks, payment rails, surveyor portals, and document stores through governed APIs and batch fallbacks.
4. **Privacy is an operating practice.** The platform uses purpose-bound data access, tokenised identifiers, role-based permissions, retention schedules, and auditable exports. This corpus contains no real personal or policy data.

## Scale snapshot: FY 2026–27 planning view

- 36 platform and domain specialists across six Indian cities
- 36 client agreements, split across all eight product lines
- 14 supported interface languages for customer communications and operator prompts
- 99.9% monthly availability objective for production services, excluding announced maintenance windows
- Three delivery patterns: API-led enterprise deployment, managed operations workspace, and advisor-assisted marketplace

Nivara deliberately measures success in completed, understandable insurance work: a policy issued with a clear premium explanation, a claim progressed with evidence intact, or a renewal surfaced before it becomes a stressful surprise.
