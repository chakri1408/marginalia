## 1. Purpose and deployment scope

Navajeevan Benefit Society appoints Nivara to provide the **Standard** configuration of JeevanPravaah for group-affinity life enrolment and persistency servicing for member communities. The initial operating territory is Maharashtra, Karnataka, and Tamil Nadu. The parties expect the deployment to support supporting respectful follow-up before cover lapses.

The agreed integration landscape includes membership roster import, communication gateway, and policy ledger export. Any connection that requires a third-party approval, source-system change, or additional security review will be tracked as a named implementation dependency rather than assumed to be available.

## 2. Term and commercial schedule

The initial term is **18 months** from the effective date. Subscription fees are **₹24.0 lakh per year**, invoiced **half-yearly in advance**, exclusive of applicable taxes. One-time discovery, configuration, migration, or travel work—if agreed—will be described in a separately approved statement of work.

At least 60 days before the term ends, the Account Director will convene a renewal review covering usage, service performance, planned scope changes, and pricing for a further period. Either party may decline renewal by written notice at least 45 days before expiry.

## 3. Delivery and acceptance

Configuration includes discovery, a mapped non-production environment, scenario-based user acceptance, launch rehearsal, and a stabilisation period. Nivara will provide training for agreed Client users and documented runbooks.

The Client will provide timely access to authorised subject-matter experts, test users, approved policy rules, and non-production interface materials. Acceptance is based on the mutually agreed scenario set, which must include incomplete evidence, manual referral, duplicate-event, and interface-delay cases relevant to this scope.

## 4. Service and governance

Standard service coverage is **09:30–18:00 IST on business days**. High-severity production incidents are acknowledged through the agreed support channel and handled according to the incident runbook. Planned maintenance and release windows will be communicated in advance wherever reasonably possible.

A monthly operating review will examine queue ageing, interface health, support themes, and planned changes. A quarterly business review will add adoption, roadmap, and commercial-reconciliation discussion.

## 5. Data, security, and confidentiality

Access is role-based, client-administered where appropriate, and logged. The parties will use fictional or de-identified material for training and non-production demonstrations; no real personal or policy identifier belongs in this corpus.

The Client controls its policy, customer, and operational source records. Nivara processes data only for the configured service and agreed support activities. Neither party may use the other party's confidential information outside this agreement without written permission, except where required by applicable law.

## 6. Product-specific commitment

During the first operating year, the parties will prioritise **a member-language preference register**. This priority is a service-design commitment, not a guarantee of an insurance outcome, claim decision, underwriting decision, or regulatory approval. Any material product expansion follows Nivara's documented change-control process.

## 7. Limitation and closure

To the extent permitted by applicable law, each party's direct liability under this fictional reference agreement is limited to subscription fees paid or payable in the preceding 12 months. Neither party is liable for indirect or consequential loss. On termination, the parties will follow an agreed export, access-removal, and transition plan.
