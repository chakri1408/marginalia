## Purpose

DaavaSaarthi is Nivara's workspace for claims intake, evidence management, assessment routing, customer communication, and settlement coordination. It is designed for insurers, TPAs, surveyor networks, repair partners, and third-party claims administrators. The product respects the fact that a polished screen is only one part of an insurance outcome: operators need explainable rules, customers need understandable next steps, and partner systems need reliable hand-offs.

## India-specific operating context

The product uses insurer-approved rules and workflows that can take account of event geography, policy conditions, preferred communication language, evidence type, service-provider network, and large-event surge protocols. It supports configurable Hindi and regional-language communications, document-led exceptions, and assisted channels for customers who begin a journey with an advisor, branch colleague, call-centre agent, or field partner.

No production customer data, government identifier, or real insurer content appears in this reference corpus.

## Core capabilities

1. **Omnichannel first-notice-of-loss capture for motor, health, property, and commercial claims.**
2. **Evidence checklists tailored to claim type, with duplicate and incomplete-file indicators.**
3. **Surveyor, repairer, and investigator assignments with clear turnaround commitments.**
4. **Customer timelines that distinguish received, under review, action needed, approved, and paid stages.**
5. **Surge command workspaces for floods, cyclones, hail, and high-volume regional incidents.**

## Controls and service design

- Role permissions separate customer support, underwriting or assessment, finance, client administration, and Nivara support duties.
- Rule changes require an accountable client approver, test evidence, an effective date, and a rollback path.
- Operational dashboards surface ageing, incomplete evidence, queue volume, interface failure, and manual override themes.
- Data exports are client-controlled and logged. Training and demonstration environments use fictional or de-identified material.
- Monthly service reviews connect product telemetry with user feedback and exception patterns; quarterly reviews prioritise roadmap changes.
