## 1. Purpose and deployment scope

Sahyadri Mobility Insurance appoints Nivara to provide the **Enterprise** configuration of VaahanSetu for commercial-vehicle and taxi policy journeys with branch-assisted servicing. The initial operating territory is Karnataka, Goa, and Telangana. The parties expect the deployment to support making usage and vehicle-class exceptions visible to underwriters.

The agreed integration landscape includes vehicle master sync, branch-user directory, and document repository. Any connection that requires a third-party approval, source-system change, or additional security review will be tracked as a named implementation dependency rather than assumed to be available.

## 2. Term and commercial schedule

The initial term is **36 months** from the effective date. Subscription fees are **₹42.0 lakh per year**, invoiced **monthly in advance**, exclusive of applicable taxes. One-time discovery, configuration, migration, or travel work—if agreed—will be described in a separately approved statement of work.

At least 60 days before the term ends, the Account Director will convene a renewal review covering usage, service performance, planned scope changes, and pricing for a further period. Either party may decline renewal by written notice at least 45 days before expiry.

## 3. Delivery and acceptance

Implementation starts with a joint workflow workshop, then proceeds through data mapping, configuration, testing of ordinary and exception scenarios, readiness sign-off, and a managed hypercare period.

The Client will provide timely access to authorised subject-matter experts, test users, approved policy rules, and non-production interface materials. Acceptance is based on the mutually agreed scenario set, which must include incomplete evidence, manual referral, duplicate-event, and interface-delay cases relevant to this scope.

## 4. Service and governance

Standard service coverage is **08:00–20:00 IST on business days**. High-severity production incidents are acknowledged through the agreed support channel and handled according to the incident runbook. Planned maintenance and release windows will be communicated in advance wherever reasonably possible.

Nivara and the Client will nominate operational owners before configuration begins. They will review acceptance evidence before production access is enabled and keep a shared decision log for controlled changes.

## 5. Data, security, and confidentiality

Nivara will apply purpose-bound access, encrypted transfer paths, retention controls, and audit logging appropriate to the configured service. The Client remains responsible for its user approvals and source-data authority.

The Client controls its policy, customer, and operational source records. Nivara processes data only for the configured service and agreed support activities. Neither party may use the other party's confidential information outside this agreement without written permission, except where required by applicable law.

## 6. Product-specific commitment

During the first operating year, the parties will prioritise **a pilot for shared-mobility fleet endorsements**. This priority is a service-design commitment, not a guarantee of an insurance outcome, claim decision, underwriting decision, or regulatory approval. Any material product expansion follows Nivara's documented change-control process.

## 7. Limitation and closure

To the extent permitted by applicable law, each party's direct liability under this fictional reference agreement is limited to subscription fees paid or payable in the preceding 12 months. Neither party is liable for indirect or consequential loss. On termination, the parties will follow an agreed export, access-removal, and transition plan.
