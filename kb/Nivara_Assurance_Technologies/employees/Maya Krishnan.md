**Team:** Product & Design

**Primary interface:** all eight products

Turns field research and client-operating evidence into a portfolio roadmap; she convenes the weekly decision clinic. Their current attention is on making sure a fast digital path remains recoverable when a customer needs human help.

## How Maya contributes

In practice, this means combining product discovery, service blueprints, and prioritisation with patient cross-functional work. They collaborate with client counterparts, Nivara colleagues, and product specialists early enough to turn ambiguity into an owned decision rather than a late-stage escalation.

A question that guides their work is: *“What becomes harder for a real operator if we remove this step?”* The question is deliberately practical. It helps the team test whether a proposed change can survive the real conditions of policy servicing, claims handling, system downtime, or a busy client launch.

## Background, designation and remit

**Formal designation:** Chief Product Officer and Co-founder

### Career background

Before co-founding Nivara, Maya spent her career translating research from call centres, branch teams, and customers into service improvements for regulated products. She learned early that the most elegant journey on a whiteboard can fail if it ignores the person asked to explain an exception.

### Current remit

She sets portfolio priorities, chairs product reviews, and protects time for discovery before a team commits to a build. Her remit includes product management, service design, accessibility, and the quality of product decisions across all eight platform lines.

### Working relationships

Maya works most closely with Aditi on client commitments, Samar on platform trade-offs, and the product managers on field evidence. She is the founding team's voice for the operator and customer who will encounter the work after launch.
